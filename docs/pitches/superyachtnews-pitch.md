Hello [Editor],

I'm Jack MacNally, director of Foreland Marine. I'm writing to propose a contributed piece for SuperyachtNews on a structural problem in the new build market that gets discussed privately at every yard meeting and rarely in print: the conflicts of interest embedded in owner's representation when the role is provided by a firm with adjacent revenue from brokers, yards, or suppliers.

The angle I'd like to take is straightforward. The Yacht Owner's Representative Register and SYBAss accreditation exist precisely because the gap is structural, not anecdotal. The strongest documented case on the public record is the one Jack Inglis at ULTIMAR set out in Superyacht Investor: a semi-custom new build where the family-office reported around 100 snags and the actual delivery list ran past 1,200. Vessels poorly overseen during construction, in his framing, return to the yards for warranty works quicker than they are delivered. That is a structural failure of the owner's representative model as it is most commonly sold, not an individual one.

The piece I'd write would do three things in roughly 1,200 to 1,500 words:

1. Lay out the four common structural conflicts in owner's representation today (brokerage arms, yard referral fees, post-delivery management contracts, preferred-supplier kickbacks) without naming firms.
2. Explain why the SYBAss / YORR framework was set up to address them and where the framework still falls short.
3. Offer a five-question diligence test owners can apply to any prospective owner's representative, including Foreland.

This would not be a marketing piece for Foreland. The reason for the byline is that we are structurally independent (no brokerage, no yard fees, no commissions), which makes it easier to write about the problem than for firms with adjacent revenue. SuperyachtNews readers will recognise the problem instantly. Few of your readers, in my experience, would say the current state of disclosure in this part of the industry is satisfactory.

I'd be happy to write to a brief of your choosing if the angle above isn't right. Word count, deadline, and any house-style requirements all flexible. I can have a complete draft to you within ten working days of confirmation.

Credentials: Foreland Marine is a SYBAss accredited owner's representative, registered on the Yacht Owner's Representative Register, with engagements across new build and refit for sailing and motor yachts from 24 to 60 metres, including J Class. We are members of British Marine and Superyacht UK. The team profile is at https://forelandmarine.com/about and the owner's representation service is at https://forelandmarine.com/owners-representation.

If a piece for SuperyachtNews isn't currently the right fit but you have an editorial brief somewhere on the year's calendar where independent industry commentary would help, I'm equally happy to be approached.
