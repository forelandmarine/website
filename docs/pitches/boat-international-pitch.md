**Recipient**: BOAT International magazine editorial team. The most direct route is the editor of BOAT Pro (the trade-facing arm) for a commercial piece, or the features editor at BOAT International for a buyer-education piece. Look at the most recent issue masthead to confirm the current names. Likely starting points: editor@boatinternational.com and contact via the Pro side at boatpro.boatinternational.com.
**Subject line**: Contributed piece: what an owner's representative actually does (and what it costs)
**Format**: cold pitch, owner-facing angle.

---

Hello [Editor],

I'm Jack MacNally, director of Foreland Marine, and I'd like to propose a contributed piece for BOAT International on a topic the magazine's owner readership consistently has to figure out for themselves: what an owner's representative actually does in a new build or refit, what it costs, and how to tell a structurally independent firm from a brokerage with a technical department in the same group.

The angle is owner-education, not industry inside-baseball. First-time buyers in particular arrive at a new build conversation having already met three or four firms who present themselves as owner's representatives. They do not know that the title is used loosely. They do not know that the firms charging one to three percent of contract value as a fixed fee, paid only by the owner, are operating on a different commercial model from the firms charging less than that but earning adjacent commissions from yards or brokers. They do not know which questions to ask to surface the difference. The piece I'd write would give them the diligence framework they need before signing an engagement letter for one of the most expensive professional services they will ever buy.

Proposed structure, around 1,500 to 2,000 words:

1. The role in one sentence and what makes it different from a project manager or a broker.
2. The five jobs in a new build (yard selection, contract negotiation, build supervision, sea trials and delivery, warranty period).
3. The fee question, answered honestly: typical range, the structures to be wary of, and why fixed-scope fees paid by the owner are the only structure that aligns incentives.
4. When to hire one (and when you can probably get away without one).
5. Five diligence questions every owner should ask any prospective owner's representative.

The piece would carry the BOAT International voice: declarative, specific, no jargon for jargon's sake. It would not be a Foreland marketing piece. Your readers will see through that immediately. The reason for the byline is that Foreland is a structurally independent owner's representative (SYBAss accredited, YORR registered, no brokerage or yard fees), which gives the piece its credibility and the specifics on fee structure their authority.

Foreland Marine works on sailing and motor yacht new builds and refits from 24 to 60 metres, with particular depth in performance sailing yachts, J Class, and carbon composite construction. Service page at https://forelandmarine.com/owners-representation.

I am equally happy to write to a brief you set if the angle above is not the one you'd like to commission. Word count, deadline, and house style all flexible. Draft within ten working days of confirmation.

I can also offer the magazine a separate, parallel piece pitched at the buyer-education end: a deeper dive into the SYBAss / YORR framework, what the accreditation actually tests for, and where it still falls short. That one would suit BOAT Pro more than the consumer book.

## Notes for sending

- BOAT International runs both the consumer magazine and BOAT Pro (trade facing). The pitch above leads with the consumer angle and offers the trade angle as an alternative. Send to the consumer features editor first; if redirected, the alternative angle is already on the table.
- If they accept, the most valuable thing is a do-follow link in the byline back to https://forelandmarine.com/owners-representation. BOAT International typically accommodates this for contributed pieces. If they don't, the piece is still worth running for the entity reinforcement.
- Mention specific recent projects only if you have the owner's permission to anonymise and reference them. If not, leave that paragraph out and say "engagements across J Class and performance sailing yachts in the 24 to 60 metre range" instead.
