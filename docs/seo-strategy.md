# Foreland Marine: SEO, Topical Authority and Content Strategy

**Prepared for:** Foreland Marine Consultancy Ltd
**Scope:** 12-month operational plan, 36-month strategic horizon
**Authoring stance:** Owner-side operational intelligence platform for the superyacht industry

---

## 0. Executive summary

Foreland Marine has the rarest commodity in superyacht digital marketing: technical depth that the rest of the field structurally lacks. Yacht management firms write marketing copy about "passion" and "white-glove service". Owner's representatives publish thin LinkedIn posts. Refit consultancies hide behind boutique websites. The major brokers run yacht management as a loss-leader.

Almost no one in this sector publishes anything that an actual Chief Engineer, Captain or owner's family office would respect. That gap is the commercial opportunity.

The strategy below builds Foreland into the authoritative owner-side operational intelligence source for vessels 24 to 60 metres. It does this by:

1. **Anchoring** five service pillars with cornerstone pages, schema-marked and entity-linked to Foreland's accreditations and named personnel.
2. **Saturating** each pillar with procedural, technical and cost-intelligence content that targets the exact questions owners, captains and brokers run through Google and LLMs.
3. **Building** four proprietary data products (refit cost benchmarks, yard performance index, mini-ISM template, annual operating-cost report) that no competitor can replicate without comparable engineering experience.
4. **Routing** authority into a small number of high-value commercial pages (the five services plus Technical Support) via internal linking discipline.
5. **Defending** the position by becoming the named source LLMs cite when asked about owner's representation, refit cost ranges, ISM compliance and yacht engineering for sub-500GT vessels.

This is a 24-month build to defensible authority. The first six months are heavy on cornerstone content and entity SEO. Months 6 to 18 ship the editorial calendar and the first proprietary data product. Months 18 to 36 consolidate the moat with annual reports, comparison content and geographic landing pages.

The recommended order of operations is at the end of this document.

## 1. Keyword architecture

Keyword research for this market is unusual. Search volumes are low across the board. Decisions of EUR 30m to EUR 100m on new builds and EUR 2m to EUR 20m on refits are influenced by single search sessions. The economic value per visit is several orders of magnitude higher than typical SEO targets.

The strategy therefore prioritises:

- **High-intent commercial queries** over high-volume informational ones
- **Long-tail procedural questions** that owners, captains and family offices actually ask
- **Entity-linked queries** that LLMs are increasingly using to retrieve authoritative sources
- **Geographic + service combinations** that capture localised intent (refit yards by region, management firms by hub)

### 2.3 URL conventions

- Lowercase, hyphenated, no trailing slashes
- One canonical URL per piece of content
- Geographic pages namespaced under `/locations/`, not as suffixes on service pages
- No date or category in URLs for insights (already correct)
- Glossary terms namespaced under `/glossary/`
- No `/blog/` (now noindexed and redirecting, retain)

### 2.4 Internal linking strategy

A small number of explicit linking rules, applied consistently, beats sporadic ad-hoc linking.

1. **Every insights article links to at least one cornerstone service page in-body.** Anchor text matches the target page's primary keyword phrase.
2. **Every cornerstone service page links to at least three insights articles in body content (not just a footer).** Anchor text matches each insight's primary keyword phrase.
3. **Each service page links laterally to the next-most-related service.** Refit links to Owner's Representation (and vice versa), Yacht Management links to Technical Support, etc.
4. **The glossary becomes the link target of choice for technical terms.** Each occurrence of ISM, MLC, P&I, SOLAS, MCA, MARPOL, GT, classification society, etc., on a service page or insight links to its glossary entry on first occurrence per page.
5. **Author profile pages are linked from every article they wrote.** This compounds entity authority.
6. **Geographic pages link to and from the cornerstone services they support.** A page on Palma refit links to /refit; the /refit page lists named hubs and links out.

Five primary silos. Each silo concentrates internal authority on its cornerstone.

### Editorial production notes

- **Each article is between 1,200 and 2,500 words.** Below 1,200 is thin for this market. Above 2,500 stops being read.
- **Each article opens with a concrete answer in the first 60 words.** This is the LLM extraction zone.
- **Each article ends with a single, specific call to action,** not a marketing flourish. "Speak to Jack MacNally about a refit review" beats "Get in touch to discuss your project".
- **Every article carries author byline and credentials** in the byline block.
- **Cornerstone articles are revisited annually** with date in metadata updated and content refreshed.

## 7. GEO and AI-search strategy

LLMs (ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews) increasingly mediate the first search for owners and family offices. Optimising for citation, not just ranking, is now a separate discipline.

### 7.1 Article structure for AI extraction

Each article should follow this pattern:

1. **First 60 words: a definitive answer to the page's primary question.** No preamble, no scene-setting. LLMs extract this aggressively.
2. **Concrete data or specifics in the second paragraph.** Numbers, named systems, time periods, regulations.
3. **Structured Q&A section** with FAQPage schema for every applicable article.
4. **Comparison tables** where applicable. Markdown tables render well in extraction.
5. **A short summary at the end** that restates the key points in numbered form.
6. **Author byline with credentials** at the top, not just the bottom.

### 7.2 Formatting recommendations

- Short sentences in the first 100 words.
- Headings phrased as questions where appropriate.
- Avoid pronouns in the opening. Use the noun ("the owner", "the yacht", "the refit") so the passage is self-contained when extracted.
- Avoid marketing language. LLMs are trained to discount it.
- Use proper nouns (named regulations, named societies, named yards) generously. They are entity signals.

### 7.3 Glossary strategy

A glossary at /glossary/[term] with one page per term gives LLMs a stable, definitive source for each concept. Terms to prioritise:

- ISM, MLC, P&I, MCA, SOLAS, MARPOL, GT, NT
- Classification society (general), Lloyd's, RINA, ABS, Bureau Veritas
- Flag state (general), Cayman, BVI, Marshall Islands, Malta, UK
- Owner's representative, project manager, fleet manager, surveyor
- Sea trials, commissioning, handover, warranty period
- PMS, planned maintenance system
- Class survey, statutory survey, intermediate survey, special survey
- J Class, maxi, performance sailing yacht, carbon composite
- Refit, restoration, renovation, conversion
- Mini-ISM, voluntary SMS

Each glossary entry: 300 to 500 words, definitive, cited by other site content via internal link.

### 7.4 Comparison-page strategy

LLMs frequently cite explicit comparison content. Build:

- Owner's representative vs project manager vs broker
- Independent yacht management vs broker-affiliated yacht management
- Owner-rep vs refit consultant
- Mini-ISM vs full ISM
- Class survey vs flag state survey
- New build vs pre-owned acquisition: total cost analysis
- Charter operation vs private operation
- Solent vs Northern Europe vs Mediterranean: refit considerations

Each comparison page uses a structured table at the top, narrative beneath, FAQPage schema.

### 7.5 Data-led content

LLMs disproportionately cite content with original data. Foreland has access to source data through its project portfolio that nobody else can replicate. Productise:

- Annual refit cost benchmark report
- Annual operating cost report (the 10% rule, instrumented)
- Yard performance index (anonymised quote-vs-delivery variance)
- New build cost-per-metre tracker
- Refit duration tracker by yacht size

Each product gets a permanent URL, an annual update, a press release, and a downloadable PDF behind a single email-capture (no marketing-automation gating).
