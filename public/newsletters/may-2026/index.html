Two years in

Two years is short enough that this is our first letter to clients and friends, and long enough to have something worth saying.

Foreland Marine is the recognition that great teams start with great people. With collective experience broad enough to count America's Cup sailors and Master Mariners amongst the ranks, we are, by good fortune, top heavy in the knowledge department.

The company has its origin in the rebuild of the 1930 J-Class Shamrock V, the once-in-a-lifetime project that leveraged a 100-strong hive of artisans and journeymen for over 100,000 man hours to preserve the original AC challenger for years to come. Both directors have been involved in the J-Class fleet before and since, in multiple roles, and we continue to have the honour of supporting these wonderful yachts.

As our second anniversary approaches us this June, we thought it prudent to celebrate the journey thus far, and take a look into what the future holds.

What we do has not changed from the original brief; both Directors still read every email and sign every brief that goes out. New build Owner's representation, refit project management, yacht management & compliance, and technical consultancy that wins races forms our core business. Our interests are directly aligned with the owner's by structure: no broker commissions, no referral fees. What follows is the work itself.

This quarter

In late April, we were in Mallorca for the Palma International Boat Show. Not a sales week: no stand, nothing pitched. The time was devoted to meetings with existing clients and new contacts. The usual conversation of boats was enjoyed, with recurring themes of market moves, new initiatives and technologies, and a warm reception for The First Owner's Reference (more on which below).

A 33-metre sailing yacht under our management has just completed a successful Caribbean and early Mediterranean season, with both trans-Atlantic shipping legs handled in-house; the same arrangement is available to other owners on yachts up to 40 metres with our established shipping partners. Yacht management at this scale is, in practice, the work of holding together the technical reading, schedule, crew, regulatory paperwork, and the owner's expectations, all in support of the Captain. Plates spinning at all times.

Planned-maintenance provisioning work continued across yachts under our care through the same period, including a remote install of a database on a 43-metre motor yacht in Tahiti. The intent throughout is a single structured record of maintenance, spares, and class survey cycles that does not leave with the crew.

In the same period, our Safety Management System service was extended to cover full ISM compliance for commercial yachts under 3,000 gross tonnes. The work is offered end-to-end to owners running their yachts commercially, from the manual through to the audits.

In Poland, a new sailing yacht is in build for a private owner. We are engaged on the owner's side, with one of us on the ground at the yard for the construction work and representation. We also have an exciting repower underway aboard a sailing yacht in Vilanova, Spain, under our technical direction.

Two working partnerships have settled into our regular practice this quarter, both extending our value-add offering. OrcaMet, led by meteorologist Steve Carver, covers weather routing for our project and managed yachts: race, ocean crossing, delivery, performance debrief. Diverse Performance Systems handles hydraulic and PLC integration, the systems work behind deck machinery on a modern yacht. Foreland also continues to support Falmouth Marine School's Foundation degree in Superyacht Engineering, with tutorials this spring on hydraulics, navcoms, electrics, and life onboard. Having contributed to the early course material and lectures, we pass on the knowledge to the next generation of Chief Engineers, so that they are best prepared for a dynamic and ever changing technical landscape.

Our website (forelandmarine.com), marketing material and social media channels were rebranded in the same period by Faro Creative Design. Feedback has been hugely positive, with more yet to come. Who had heard of Answer Engine Optimisation (AEO) six months ago?

Technical Support

Technical Support is a new standing on-call arrangement for yachts of 24 metres and over. It exists because the difference between a problem solved in a morning and one that lingers for weeks is usually one phone call: knowing who to ask, and having that person already familiar with the yacht.

Standby covers the basics: an annual planned-maintenance audit onboard, engineer hours on call, parts sourcing at cost. Direct adds hand-carried parts and a quarterly check-in. Captive adds a pre-passage audit and an assigned lead engineer. Three tiers, from GBP 250 to GBP 1,000 a month. Full detail at forelandmarine.com/technical-support.

The First Owner's Reference

Alongside the commercial work; a soon-to-be-published independent buyer's guide. The First Owner's Reference is an annual editorial for first-time superyacht buyers, published by Foreland Marine but kept structurally separate from any commercial engagement and declared as such. The site is now live at firstownersreference.com. The first print edition is scheduled for Q4 2026.

The editorial position is the load-bearing thing. Much of the yachting trade press and glossy magazines are printed by the brokerages. This is not a criticism, rather just the mechanics of the industry. TFOR carries no advertising, sponsored content, or paid placement, enabling it to give the reader a full and free appraisal of all areas and aspects of yacht ownership without prejudice. Contributions made by leading industry voices are for the benefit of the reader, and the firms named in its chapters are featured in existing published record. The argument across the nine lead essays is that the structure of the superyacht industry places most of the firms around a first acquisition on the other side of the table from the owner, and that the right response is to engage advisers whose interests are aligned before engaging anyone aligned with someone else's. It is the kind of publication the trade press is structurally less able to publish. That is the reason it exists.

The First Owner's Reference is still being written and contributed to, ahead of print publication. We invite all of our clients and partners to read the web version and pass on their comments and queries to editors@firstownersreference.com. A collective voice for all.
