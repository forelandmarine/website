# Foreland Marine: Instagram Reels Strategy

Working document for the social media marketing manager. Updated April 2026.

---

## Target Audience Profile

**Who they are:**

- Age 28-36, skewing male but increasingly mixed
- Net worth $5M-$50M+, mostly liquid from a recent exit, token unlock, or business sale
- First-generation wealth. No family yacht, no childhood summers in Monaco. They googled "how much does a yacht cost" before they called anyone.
- Based in tech hubs (SF, Austin, Miami, London, Dubai, Singapore) but mobile. Many are already spending time in the Med or Caribbean.

**Psychographics:**

- They research everything before buying. They read forums, watch YouTube deep-dives, and compare specs in spreadsheets before making decisions.
- Suspicious of traditional luxury gatekeeping. They dislike being sold to and can smell inauthenticity immediately.
- They respect transparency, data, and expertise delivered without pretension.
- They are used to moving fast. The yacht industry's pace (18-month refits, 3-year new builds) is a culture shock.
- Status matters, but they want it to feel earned and informed, not inherited.

**What they consume:**

- YouTube: tech reviews, business breakdowns (My First Million, All-In Podcast clips), travel vlogs, "how things work" content
- Podcasts: business, investing, tech
- Instagram: architecture, design, travel, cars, watches. They follow luxury accounts but engage more with educational or behind-the-scenes content.
- Twitter/X: tech and crypto discourse, threads explaining complex topics
- TikTok: they are on it, even if they would not admit it. Short-form education and "day in the life" content pulls them in.
- Reddit: r/sailing, r/boating, r/fatFIRE, r/yacht

**Who they follow (archetypes, not specific accounts):**

- Founders who share openly about wealth decisions
- Luxury reviewers who focus on value and craftsmanship, not just flash
- Niche experts who break down opaque industries (real estate, aviation, watches)
- Travel creators who show logistics, not just the highlight reel

## Content Pillars

### Pillar 1: Myth Busting / "Things Nobody Tells You"

**Description:** Debunking common assumptions about yacht ownership. Revealing hidden costs, industry practices, and uncomfortable truths that brokers and yards would rather keep quiet.

**Why it works:** This audience has a low tolerance for being blindsided. They want to feel like insiders before they commit. Content that says "here is what the industry does not want you to know" triggers their research instinct and builds trust instantly.

**Reel ideas:**

1. **"A $5M yacht costs $500K-$1M a year to run. Here's where that money actually goes."**
   Format: Talking head with cost breakdown graphics appearing on screen. End with CTA to the Running Cost Calculator.

2. **"Your yacht broker works for the seller, not you. Here's why that matters."**
   Format: Talking head, direct to camera. Conversational, slightly conspiratorial tone.

3. **"The yacht listing says 'excellent condition.' Here's what we found during survey."**
   Format: POV walk-through of a yacht with visible issues. Text overlay calling out each problem.

4. **"Why your yacht will lose 30-40% of its value in the first 5 years, and why that might not matter."**
   Format: Talking head with simple chart graphic. Frame it as a comparison to private jet depreciation.

5. **"No, you cannot just park your yacht wherever you want. Marina berths are harder to get than restaurant reservations in Monaco."**
   Format: Quick talking head, punchy delivery. Show footage of packed marinas.

6. **"The refit quote said 6 months. It took 14. Here's what went wrong."**
   Format: Before/during/after b-roll with text overlay narration. Real project footage.

7. **"Everyone tells you to buy a yacht. Nobody tells you about flag state regulations."**
   Format: Talking head with on-screen bullet points. Keep it brief, position it as "the boring stuff that saves you millions."

8. **"The crew you hire matters more than the yacht you buy."**
   Format: Talking head. Give 2-3 real examples of how crew quality impacts the ownership experience.

### Pillar 2: Behind the Scenes

**Description:** Raw, real footage from active projects. Refit progress, yard visits, sea trials, technical inspections, deliveries. Showing the actual work that goes into yacht ownership and management.

**Why it works:** This audience lives on behind-the-scenes content. They watched factory tours and "how it's made" videos long before they could afford yachts. Real project footage is inherently interesting and positions Foreland as practitioners, not just advisors.

**Reel ideas:**

1. **"Day 1 vs Day 180 of a full yacht refit."**
   Format: Split screen or quick-cut timelapse. Dramatic before/after with minimal text.

2. **"What a yacht survey actually looks like (it's not glamorous)."**
   Format: POV footage of crawling through engine rooms, checking hull thickness, opening panels. Voiceover explaining what you are looking for.

3. **"Sea trial day. Here's what we test and why."**
   Format: B-roll from a real sea trial. Text overlay listing each test. Show the instruments, the wake, the engine room under load.

4. **"This 45m yacht hasn't moved in 2 years. Here's what we found underneath."**
   Format: Underwater footage or haul-out footage showing marine growth, paint condition. Genuinely fascinating visuals.

5. **"Walking a shipyard at 6am before the owners arrive."**
   Format: Quiet, atmospheric POV walk through a yard. Early morning light, yachts in various states of undress. Minimal text, let the visuals carry it.

6. **"The moment a freshly painted hull hits the water."**
   Format: Launch footage. Satisfying, shareable. Simple text overlay with yacht specs.

7. **"Every tool in a yacht surveyor's bag, explained in 60 seconds."**
   Format: Flat lay or quick-fire show-and-tell. Educational and oddly satisfying.

8. **"This is what $2M of yacht refit looks like."**
   Format: Quick montage of the scope of work. New teak decks, engine rebuild, paint, interior refit. Each shot with a cost callout.

### Pillar 3: First-Timer Education

**Description:** Practical, no-nonsense guidance for people entering yacht ownership for the first time. What to look for, what to ask, what order to do things in, and what mistakes to avoid.

**Why it works:** This audience is smart but new to yachting. They do not want to look stupid, and they do not want to be patronized. Straightforward educational content that respects their intelligence while filling genuine knowledge gaps is exactly what they are searching for.

**Reel ideas:**

1. **"Buying your first superyacht? Do these 5 things before you call a broker."**
   Format: Talking head, numbered list with text overlay. CTA to the "How to Buy Your First Superyacht" blog post.

2. **"Sail vs motor: here's how to actually decide."**
   Format: Side-by-side footage of both. Talking head breaking down the real trade-offs (not just aesthetics).

3. **"What size yacht do you actually need? A brutally honest guide."**
   Format: Talking head with footage of different yacht sizes. Frame it around use case, not ego.

4. **"New build vs pre-owned: the decision that will save or cost you millions."**
   Format: Talking head with pros/cons appearing as text overlay. Direct, no hedging.

5. **"The 3 questions I ask every first-time buyer."**
   Format: Talking head, close-up, conversational. Positions Foreland as the trusted advisor.

6. **"Yacht management explained in 60 seconds. What it is, what it costs, and why you need it."**
   Format: Talking head with supporting b-roll of management activities (maintenance, crew management, provisioning).

7. **"Your first yacht season: a realistic timeline from purchase to your first trip."**
   Format: Timeline graphic with talking head. Show that it takes longer than people expect, but frame it positively.

8. **"How to read a yacht listing like a professional."**
   Format: Screen recording of an actual listing (anonymized if needed). Point out what matters, what is marketing fluff, and what is a red flag.

### Pillar 4: Lifestyle Without the Fluff

**Description:** The real ownership experience. Yes, the beautiful anchorages and the sunset dinners, but also the 3am engine alarm, the refit that ran over budget, the insurance renewal that made your eyes water. Authentic, balanced content.

**Why it works:** This audience is saturated with aspirational yacht content and they find it hollow. Showing the full picture, the good and the unglamorous, is a differentiator. It also pre-qualifies leads: people who watch this content and still want to buy are serious buyers.

**Reel ideas:**

1. **"Nobody posts this part of yacht ownership."**
   Format: Quick montage of unglamorous moments: bilge pumps, paperwork stacks, waiting for parts, yard dust everywhere. Set to a trending sound that contrasts with the visuals.

2. **"Expectation vs reality: your first yacht refit."**
   Format: Side-by-side. Left: glossy renders and timelines. Right: delayed deliveries, unexpected corrosion, budget revisions.

3. **"A day in the life of a yacht manager (it's not what you think)."**
   Format: Day-in-the-life format. Show the range: supplier calls at 7am, yard meetings, solving a plumbing issue, reviewing accounts, then maybe 10 minutes of nice scenery at the end.

4. **"The most underrated part of owning a yacht isn't the yacht."**
   Format: Talking head. The answer is the people: crew who become family, the community, the places you would never visit otherwise.

5. **"What I wish every owner knew before their first Med season."**
   Format: Talking head, list format. Practical tips: book berths early, carry cash for small ports, your captain's local knowledge is everything.

6. **"The real reason people sell their yachts (it's not what you'd guess)."**
   Format: Talking head. Address the taboo. Common reasons: lifestyle change, underuse, cost fatigue, divorce. Normalize it.

### Pillar 5: Quick Data / Calculator Teasers

**Description:** Short, punchy, stat-driven content. "Did you know" facts, cost comparisons, and direct hooks into the Running Cost Calculator tool on the website.

**Why it works:** This audience thinks in numbers. They are used to evaluating investments, comparing unit economics, and making data-driven decisions. Quick stats feel native to how they already process information. These reels also have high share potential because they pack value into a few seconds.

**Reel ideas:**

1. **"How much does a 40m yacht actually cost per hour of use?"**
   Format: Text on screen with simple math. Talking head reveal. CTA: "Run your own numbers with our calculator, link in bio."

2. **"Yacht fuel costs, visualized."**
   Format: Graphic showing fuel burn at different speeds for different yacht sizes. Quick, clean, shareable.

3. **"The most expensive 5 words in yachting: 'While you're at it, could you...'"**
   Format: Talking head, quick and funny. Explain how scope creep in refits adds up. Show a real example.

4. **"$30M yacht. $0 in the bank. Here's how yacht financing actually works."**
   Format: Talking head with simple graphics. Cover deposit, loan terms, and the role of charter income.

5. **"I ran the numbers on chartering vs owning. Here's the break-even."**
   Format: Screen recording of a spreadsheet or the calculator tool. Walk through the math quickly.

6. **"Insurance on a $20M yacht costs more per year than most people's house."**
   Format: Talking head with stat on screen. Quick, memorable, shareable.

7. **"Crew costs for a 40m yacht: a full breakdown."**
   Format: List format with roles and salary ranges appearing on screen. End with total annual crew cost.

## Posting Cadence

**Frequency:** 4-5 reels per week.

This is an aggressive but sustainable pace if you batch-produce content. Film 2-3 talking head sessions per month (10-15 reels per session). Supplement with b-roll reels from yard visits and projects as they happen.

**Best posting times for this demographic:**

- Weekdays: 7:00-8:30am (morning scroll before work) and 6:00-8:00pm (evening wind-down)
- Weekends: 9:00-11:00am
- All times should be set to the primary target timezone. If the audience is split between US and Europe, default to GMT/UK time for the first 90 days, then adjust based on analytics.

**Content mix (per week of 5 reels):**

| Pillar | Reels per week | Notes |
|--------|---------------|-------|
| Myth Busting | 1 | Highest share potential. Prioritize this pillar. |
| Behind the Scenes | 1-2 | Depends on available footage. Batch when visiting yards. |
| First-Timer Education | 1 | Steady, reliable performer. Drives blog traffic. |
| Lifestyle Without the Fluff | 1 | Keeps the feed human and relatable. |
| Quick Data / Calculator | 0-1 | Use as a wildcard. Best for driving website clicks. |

Rotate so no two consecutive reels are from the same pillar.

## Production Guidelines

### Video Length

- **Sweet spot: 30-60 seconds.** Long enough to deliver value, short enough to hold attention.
- **Under 15 seconds:** Use only for punchy data reels or satisfying b-roll moments.
- **60-90 seconds:** Use sparingly, only for behind-the-scenes walkthroughs or detailed myth-busting. The content must earn every second.
- Never exceed 90 seconds. If it needs more time, make it a two-part series.

### Aspect Ratio and Formatting

- **9:16 vertical, 1080x1920px.** Always. No exceptions.
- Keep text within the safe zone (away from the top and bottom 250px where UI elements overlap).
- Use a consistent text style: clean sans-serif font, white or off-white, with a subtle shadow or background bar for legibility.
- Include the Foreland Marine logo as a small, unobtrusive watermark in one corner. Not a full brand intro.

### Music and Audio Strategy

- **Talking head reels:** Use original audio (the speaker's voice). Add a low, ambient background track if the recording environment has distracting noise.
- **B-roll reels:** Use trending sounds when they genuinely fit. Do not force a trending sound onto content where it feels unnatural. Cinematic ambient tracks (ocean, engine hum, yard noise) work well for yacht content.
- **Voiceover reels:** Record a clean voiceover in a quiet space. Pair with b-roll. This format works well for myth-busting and education.
- **General rule:** Original audio and voiceover perform better for building authority. Trending sounds perform better for reach. Aim for a 60/40 split toward original audio.

### Caption and Hashtag Strategy

**Captions:**

- First line: restate the hook or add a complementary angle. This is the line people see before tapping "more."
- Body: 2-4 short sentences expanding on the reel's point. Add one detail that did not fit in the video.
- End with a clear CTA (see CTA patterns below).
- Keep total caption length under 150 words.

**Hashtags:**

Use 5-8 hashtags per post. Mix of:

- **Niche:** #superyacht #yachtrefit #yachtmanagement #yachtowner #yachtlife
- **Audience-adjacent:** #luxurylifestyle #firsttimebuyer #wealthbuilding
- **Discovery:** #thingsiwishiknew #didyouknow #behindthescenes
- Avoid generic tags like #luxury #ocean #boat. They attract the wrong audience.
- Place hashtags at the end of the caption, not in the first line.

### CTA Patterns

Rotate between these. Do not use the same CTA on consecutive posts.

| CTA Type | When to use | Example |
|----------|-------------|---------|
| Drive to blog | Education and myth-busting reels | "Full guide in the link in bio: How to Buy Your First Superyacht." |
| Drive to calculator | Data/cost reels | "Run your own numbers. Calculator link in bio." |
| Drive engagement | Behind the scenes, lifestyle | "What would you want to see on a yard visit? Drop it in the comments." |
| Drive follows | High-performing reels | "Follow for more honest yacht content." |
| Drive saves | High-value education | "Save this for when you're ready to start looking." |

Aim for 40% engagement CTAs, 40% website CTAs, 20% follow/save CTAs.

## Hook Formulas

These are the first 1-3 seconds of the reel. They determine whether someone keeps watching. Every reel must open with a strong hook.

### Templates

1. **"Nobody tells you this about [topic]."**
   Example: "Nobody tells you this about yacht insurance."

2. **"The biggest mistake first-time yacht buyers make."**
   Example: (Open on a visually striking yacht, then cut to talking head delivering the answer.)

3. **"This is what [$X] gets you in yachting."**
   Example: "This is what $500K a year in running costs actually looks like."

4. **"I've managed [X] yacht refits. Here's what goes wrong every time."**
   Example: (Establishes authority immediately, then delivers specific insight.)

5. **"Stop. Don't buy a yacht until you know this."**
   Example: (Direct, slightly urgent. Works well for cost-related or legal content.)

6. **"Here's a yacht industry secret that will save you [money/time/headaches]."**
   Example: "Here's a yacht industry secret that will save you six figures on your refit."

7. **"[X] costs more than you think. Let me show you."**
   Example: "Mooring in Monaco costs more than you think. Let me show you."

8. **"You can afford the yacht. Can you afford to own it?"**
   Example: (Strong opener for any running cost content. Provocative but not condescending.)

9. **"POV: You just bought your first superyacht. Now what?"**
   Example: (Works as a series. Each reel covers the next step.)

10. **"If your broker isn't telling you this, find a new broker."**
    Example: (Positions Foreland as the honest alternative. Use carefully to avoid sounding aggressive toward competitors.)

### Opening Frame Rules

- The first frame must be visually interesting even as a still thumbnail. A face with an expressive reaction, a dramatic yacht shot, or bold text on screen.
- If using text overlay as the hook, make the font large enough to read without tapping. 3-7 words maximum on the opening frame.
- Start talking within the first second. Dead air at the top kills retention.
- Never open with "Hey guys" or "Welcome back." Start with the hook immediately.

## Cross-Promotion Strategy

### TikTok

- Repost every reel to TikTok with no changes except removing the Instagram watermark (download the original file, do not screen-record).
- TikTok favors slightly longer content (45-90 seconds) and raw/unpolished aesthetics. The behind-the-scenes and myth-busting pillars will likely perform best here.
- Use TikTok-specific hashtags and sounds where relevant.
- TikTok's audience skews younger but overlaps with the target demo more than people assume, especially in finance and entrepreneurship niches.

### YouTube Shorts

- Repost all reels as Shorts. YouTube Shorts has lower competition in the yacht/marine niche and strong discoverability.
- Add a verbal CTA to "check the full video on our channel" if you plan to create long-form YouTube content later.

### LinkedIn

- Repost 2-3 reels per week to LinkedIn as native video. Focus on the education, myth-busting, and data pillars. Skip the lifestyle content.
- LinkedIn captions should be longer and more professional in tone. Add a paragraph of context that frames the reel as industry insight.
- This is where you reach the audience's advisors, family offices, and professional networks.

### Connecting Reels to Blog Content and the Calculator

- Every education and myth-busting reel should end with a reference to deeper content on the website.
- Create a Linktree or single landing page (link in bio) with clear paths to:
  - "How to Buy Your First Superyacht" blog post
  - Running Cost Calculator
  - Contact/consultation booking
- When a reel covers a topic the blog post addresses in detail, say so explicitly: "I break this down step by step in the full guide. Link in bio."
- For calculator reels, show the calculator on screen (screen recording) so viewers know what they are clicking through to.

### Instagram Stories (Complementing Reels)

- Repost every reel to Stories on the day it publishes. Add a poll, question box, or "tap to read more" sticker to drive interaction.
- Use Stories for day-of content that does not warrant a reel: quick yard photos, "currently working on" updates, sharing relevant news articles.
- Create a set of Story Highlights:
  - "Start Here" (pinned intro, links to key resources)
  - "Refit Diary" (ongoing project updates)
  - "Costs Explained" (calculator and data content)
  - "FAQs" (answers to common DM questions)
- Post 3-5 Stories per day on active days. Even on non-reel days, post at least 1-2 Stories to stay visible in the tray.

## KPIs and Measurement

### Metrics to Track Weekly

| Metric | What it tells you |
|--------|-------------------|
| Reel views (plays) | Reach and discoverability |
| Average watch time (%) | Content quality and hook effectiveness |
| Saves | High-value signal. People save content they plan to revisit. |
| Shares | Virality and relevance. Shares drive reach more than likes. |
| Comments | Engagement depth. Reply to every comment in the first hour. |
| Profile visits from reels | Funnel health. Are viewers curious enough to check the profile? |
| Link clicks (link in bio) | Direct website traffic. The ultimate conversion metric for this strategy. |
| Follower growth rate | Long-term audience building. |

### Benchmarks (Niche B2C, Luxury/Marine, 0-10K Followers)

These are starting benchmarks. Adjust after 90 days of data.

- **Views per reel:** 500-2,000 in the first 48 hours (this will vary wildly; focus on consistency, not individual outliers)
- **Average watch time:** 40%+ is good, 60%+ is excellent
- **Engagement rate (likes + comments + saves + shares / reach):** 5-10% for a small, niche account
- **Saves-to-views ratio:** 2-5% is strong
- **Shares-to-views ratio:** 1-3% is strong
- **Weekly follower growth:** 2-5% of current follower count
- **Link clicks per week:** Track baseline in month 1, aim for 10-15% growth month over month

### Monthly Review

On the first Monday of each month:

1. Export analytics for all reels published that month.
2. Identify top 3 and bottom 3 reels by views, watch time, and saves.
3. Look for patterns: which pillar, format, hook style, and posting time performed best?
4. Adjust the next month's content mix based on findings.
5. Review website analytics: how much traffic came from Instagram? Which pages did they visit? Did anyone use the calculator or read the blog post?
6. Document learnings in a running log so patterns become visible over quarters, not just months.

## First 30 Days Launch Plan

### Pre-Launch (Before Day 1)

- Set up the Instagram profile: clear bio, profile photo (logo or branded headshot), link in bio pointing to a landing page with the blog post and calculator.

### Week 1 (Days 1-7): Launch and Establish Tone

**Goal:** Publish 4 reels. Establish who Foreland is and what this account is about.

**Reel 2 (Day 3) - First-Timer Education**
- Hook: "Buying your first superyacht? Do these 5 things before you call a broker."

(3) Research flag states and registration. (4) Talk to an independent consultant, not a broker, first. (5) Visit 3 shipyards before you commit to one.

**Reel 15 (Day 26) - Myth Busting**
- Hook: "You can afford the yacht. Can you afford to own it?"
- Format: Talking head. Cover the gap between purchase price and total cost of ownership. Use the "10% rule" (annual running costs are roughly 10% of the yacht's value) as a starting framework, then explain why it is a rough guide at best.
- CTA: "See what it would actually cost you. Calculator in bio."
- Length: 45-55 seconds.

**Reel 16 (Day 28) - First-Timer Education**
- Hook: "POV: You just bought your first superyacht. Now what?"
- Format: Talking head or voiceover with b-roll. Walk through the first 30 days of ownership: flag registration, crew hiring, insurance, first maintenance checks, provisioning, maiden voyage planning.
- CTA: "This is step 1 of a series. Follow so you don't miss the rest."
- Length: 60 seconds.

**Reel 17 (Day 30) - Lifestyle Without the Fluff**
- Hook: "The most underrated part of owning a yacht isn't the yacht."
- Format: Talking head, warm and genuine. Talk about the community, the crew relationships, the places you would never visit without a boat. End on a real, human note.
- CTA: "If this resonated, share it with someone who's thinking about it."
- Length: 45-55 seconds.
